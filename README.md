This repository contains the analysis code for a multi-tissue profiling study of early-stage metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD). The study aims to define the clonal architecture and functional relevance of T cells during the earliest stages of MASLD, when immune-mediated processes are most likely to influence progression to liver fibrosis.

The dataset comprises **80 tissue samples from 20 patients undergoing bariatric surgery**, including **matched liver tissue, peripheral blood mononuclear cell (PBMC), subcutaneous adipose tissue (SAT), and visceral adipose tissue (VAT)**, ranging from Healthy liver through steatosis and mild-to-moderate fibrosis.

**CITE seq** was performed to capture transcriptomic and surface protein phenotypes, together with **T cell receptor (TCR) sequencing** to resolve clonal relationships of T cells across tissues. To assess disease regression and immune remodelling, four individuals underwent **longitudinal liver fine‑needle aspiration (FNA), with paired baseline and follow‑up samples** collected after bariatric surgery.

The analyses implemented in this repository integrate CITE seq and TCR seq data to identify **clonally expanded effector CD8⁺ T cells shared between liver and adipose tissues during early MASLD**, characterise their interactions with liver endothelial cells, and quantify changes in clonal expansion and diversity following disease regression. 

Scripts are organised by figure and analysis stage, enabling full traceability from raw sequencing outputs to the figures and results presented in the manuscript.
